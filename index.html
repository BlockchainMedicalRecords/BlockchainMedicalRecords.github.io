Project Description

Security Through Decentralization

Ensuring medical transactions and the storage of patient records can be trusted

  • 31 January, 2020

Source: DataArt

Supporting the blockchain will be a ledger which records all transactions ever made over the blockchain. Specifically, as a private consortium, we as a group will have control over the information thus ensuring all transaction history can be viewed and private data is not shared. All patient information will be stored utilizing AWS. The database system we will utilize is known as MongoDB which will store a medical chart of a patient, also known as their patient history, including: demographics, family history, social history, habits, medication, allergies, medical allergies, immunization records, surgical history, obstetric history, and medical encounters. To assist the blockchain, a subsystem that handles AWS queries will be created, ideally improving performance time.

To assist insurance and medical processes, the system will employ ethereum based transactions. Blockchain Medical Records (BMR) will display an itemized bill for hospital visits or assosciated medical expenses and provide users with the option to take care of the co-pay with their linked ethereum wallet. Through a seamless transaction process and candid expense reports, the goal is to minimize administrative pains for those in the medical sector and patients alike.
